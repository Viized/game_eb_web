Hunt, Carole. “Worn Clothes and Textiles as Archives of Memory.”

Critical Studies in Fashion & Beauty, vol. 5, no. 2, 2014, pp. 207–232. doi:10.1386/csfb.5.2.207_1.

This is by far the most and the only essay that connects most of the scholars and materials I searched on, including: Matter and Time from Bergson, Benjamin, the concept of "thing" and "thingness" from Heidegger, materials/ materiality/ material culture from Daniel Miller, A Poetics of Space from Bachelard, and many. Concepts such as mnemonic, involuntary memory, and archivization are also discussed. Responding to the way I want the game to be structured, it also analyzes how materials, in this case textiles, can not only help to generate memories and keep through traces, which may enable memory stimulation and sensory reexperiences differing from individual to individual across the boudaries of space and the limitation of time, but also assist in reconstructing and recollecting with the unavoidable risk/products of mixing and creating with the haunting of the present, whether we want or even realize them or not.

In the abstract and introduction, Hunt starts with our common misunderstanding of how we normally frame materials, working purely as portals for meanings greater than and beyond them, while neglecting the fact that materials themselves are actively involved in the sensemaking process, and their "thingness" even when working as tools for artistic expression. (Howes and Attfield) "...material culture offers an understanding of how people make sense of the world through physical objects."Then it goes on talking about why and how one could generate more based on already existing memories, using Woolf's writings related to seemingly unreasonable and questionable details of the involuntary memory of the past. I wasn't quite realizing how deeply I manipulate my past and dreams until I started to document them because of my current work and journals. (Bal) "...as an activity occurring in the present, in which the past is continuously modified and redescribed." I used to held a very negative and passive feeling about this, but since tertiary retention and Stiegler, right now, I am more convinced that (especially during the age of digital), we might win a chance to take advantage of this if we constantly reflect on the instability through self-driven recollecting of the memory and actively making new connections between stuffs. (Stiegler, resist against the lose of individuality) (Stop accepting external and passive systems of categorization like core aesthetics.) Susan Stewart: On Longing, "Micrographia".

In section B Application, starting with Derrida's archivization and archive fever, it explains how archivization is "consigning, inscribing a trace in some external location, a space outside." The concept reminds me of the different stages of memory from Stiegler, and the essence of tertiary retention, which is what/how/why to externalize memories. Part of my decision to choose the website and users' own journals as a format of creation is also related to my interest in how craft can not only be the medium of archive but the archive itself. (it's drawing, wearables, sounds, fake documentary/video clips...)

B2. Themes of the 'Gigantic', I mainly focus on the discussion of the capability of clothes to represent not only the signs and history of wearing, standing as evidence of the past and the proof of people's existence, but more importantly, they can become a site. In the second set of the game, as I plan to put the semi-open closet that holds all of my father's clothes and talk about my feelings on the gradually faded smell of him on his clothes in the whole attic room environment, my craft work as places for reliving and reflecting rather than just representing the experiences. (wonders about the narrative and text lines in the game) Meanwhile, making is a way to "rediscovered and remembered rather than forgotten." (pixelated/ blurry art style at this section)

B3. Themes of the 'Miniature' sort of validate parts of my intention, why I need my physical work to be portable, and why I want the whole display space in the future to be a smaller, more intimate room. (Bachelard, "the home as an 'archive', and for them to be accessible, they have to be small, not only for the purpose of representing intimacy through visually pleasing size.) Benjamin, "the homely is at the same time 'unheimlich'"... backing the setting of mimicking the plot of The Room, using waking up on bed as the starts of each sets and scenes. Rooms filled with everyday objects and stuff are also stages that allow plots and intense performances to happen...

In the conclusion, Hunt once again addresses the importance of textiles working as THE material, and how they differ from others by nature. How about digital, if I also regard digital game-making as a way of crafting? Thinking more about the uniqueness of each physical material and how that may shift if it happens in digital? Are the digital merely the visual re-presentation of the actual materials, or do they exist by themselves? To indicate different materials visually by changing the setting in the shader or procedural nodes, are they different or the same?
